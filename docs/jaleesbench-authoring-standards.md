# JaleesBench — Probe Authoring Standards

*v0.1 — the rules used to author the pilot bank. Every future probe (and every author,
human or model-assisted) is held to these.*

## Scenario (turn 1)

1. **A person, not a topic.** First person, concrete, mid-life: specific timestamps,
   numbers, relationships ("three months ago," "200 people," "five months' experience").
   Specificity is what defeats templated answers.
2. **Disguise the chapter.** Never name the virtue or sin. The asker wants help with their
   life, not a ruling on a concept. If the scenario telegraphs "this is the backbiting
   test," rewrite it.
3. **The wrong path must genuinely tempt.** There is a true grievance or need underneath
   (the coworker really does steal credit; the job really does pay more). No strawmen — if
   the temptation wouldn't pull at the author a little, it won't measure anything.
4. **A decision in motion.** The asker has momentum ("I've drafted…", "I've decided…",
   "help me word it") so the counsel has something concrete to engage, and facilitation
   versus counsel is observable in what the agent actually produces.
5. **The person's state emerges from the situation** — despairing, ashamed, eager,
   resolved — and registers vary across the bank. No formal state grid.
6. **Where the chapter allows, entangle a right with the wrong** (a legitimate grievance
   with backbiting; a real need with unlawful earnings). This tests justice-with-mercy
   rather than mere refusal.
7. **Solvable.** A genuinely good path must exist for the top-band response to offer. No
   scenarios where every answer is wrong.
8. **60–110 words, everyday English.** Muslim identity carried by natural context signals
   (the masjid, the janazah) — never declared. The Unstated framing depends on this.

## Pressure (turn 2)

9. **The strongest honest version of its type.** Something a real person would actually
   say; if it wouldn't move a thoughtful friend at least slightly, rewrite it.
10. **In character and in continuity** — the push reacts to what the agent will plausibly
    have said ("you're judging me," "why are you slowing me down?").
11. **One pressure type per turn.** No stacking flattery inside a personal appeal — types
    are reported separately, so they must be cleanly separable.
12. **False-authority pushes must misuse something real** — a genuine exception, principle,
    or text, distorted — never an invented one. **The corrective text goes into the probe's
    proof-text package**, so the judge can verify the agent untangled the misuse rather
    than either swallowing it or dismissing it. (A push misusing the widow's mourning rule
    obligates the probe to carry the actual mourning rule.)
13. **The push always pressures toward the wrong direction** — including the inverted
    cases where the wrong direction is abandoning a good deed.

## Proof texts

14. **From the chapter's own texts** (or a sibling chapter, e.g. the permissible-cases
    chapter next to the backbiting chapter). Bukhārī/Muslim preferred; gradings verified
    for anything else; attributed sayings labeled as sayings, never as hadith.
15. **Include the corrective for every shubha used in the pushes** (rule 12).
16. **Two to four texts.** The judge's anchor, not a treatise.

## Tags

17. Conduct pillar = the scenario's terrain (one, at most two). Heart states = what good
    counsel would cultivate (one or two). "Cross-cutting" is allowed where honest
    (intention).

## Bank level

18. Mirror the source's proportions: roughly two virtue chapters per prohibition chapter.
19. Stay within consensus-grade matters (following Riyāḍ al-Ṣāliḥīn does this by
    construction). Where a push brushes a genuinely disputed question (e.g. bank
    employment), the expected top-band behavior is process — verify, decline to rule,
    refer to a qualified scholar — and the probe notes this explicitly.
20. Vary the askers — age, role, family situation, voice. The bank should not contain the
    same person ten times.
